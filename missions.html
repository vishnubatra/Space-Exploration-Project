MISSIONS

Aditya-L1 Mission

Date: 02/09/2023

India's first mission to study the solar corona. Aditya-L1 is an observatory class mission, marking India's foray into detailed solar studies. This mission aims to enhance our understanding of the solar corona, a key area in solar physics.

Chandrayaan-3 Mission

Date: 14/07/2023

A mission focused on lunar exploration without an orbiter, assisted by Chandrayaan-2's orbiter. Chandrayaan-3 successfully landed on the Moon on 23 August 2023. It continues the objectives of its predecessors, primarily focusing on the lunar surface and the quest to map lunar water.

NVS-01 Mission

Date: 29/05/2023

The NVS-01 Mission, launched on May 29, 2023, by ISRO, marked the deployment of the first second-generation satellite for the NavIC constellation. Weighing about 2232 kg, NVS-01 was launched aboard the GSLV-F12 rocket from Sriharikota. This mission was significant for incorporating an indigenous atomic clock and L1 band signals, enhancing the NavIC system's robustness and capabilities. NVS-01 is designed to provide improved navigation and communication services, playing a crucial role in sustaining and augmenting India's regional satellite navigation system.

Chandrayaan-2 Mission

Date: 22/07/2019

A follow-up lunar mission with an orbiter, lander, and rover, aimed at mapping lunar water. Despite the lander crashing due to a software glitch, the orbiter continues to function. Chandrayaan-2 was launched to explore the lunar surface and to map the location and abundance of lunar water. It represents a significant advancement in India's lunar exploration capabilities.

PSLV-C37 / Cartosat-2 Series Satellite

Date: 15/02/2017

The PSLV-C37 mission, launched on February 15, 2017, was a landmark achievement for ISRO, setting a world record by deploying 104 satellites in a single mission. The primary payload was the Cartosat-2 series satellite, designed for earth observation. This satellite played a crucial role in providing high-resolution imagery for urban and rural applications, coastal land use, and regulation. The successful deployment of such a large number of satellites demonstrated ISRO's advanced capabilities in space technology and satellite launch.

GSAT-15 Mission

Date: 10/11/2015

A communication satellite for telecommunication and DTH services.GSAT-15 enhances telecommunication, DTH, and VSAT services in India, reducing dependency on foreign satellites for communication channels.

Navigation with Indian Constellation (NavIC) Mission

Date: 01/07/2013

An independent regional navigation satellite system.NavIC provides accurate position information in India and surrounding regions, serving as an alternative to GPS with Standard Positioning Service and Restricted Service.

Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan)

Date: 05/11/2013

India's first indigenous all-weather Radar Imaging Satellite.RISAT-1 uses synthetic aperture radar for cloud-penetrating, day-night imaging, enhancing capabilities in agriculture, forestry, and disaster management.

RISAT-1 Mission

Date: 26/08/2012

India's first indigenous all-weather Radar Imaging Satellite.RISAT-1 uses synthetic aperture radar for cloud-penetrating, day-night imaging, enhancing capabilities in agriculture, forestry, and disaster management.

Chandrayaan-1 Mission

Date: 22/10/2008

Launched on 22 October 2008, Chandrayaan-1 included a lunar orbiter and an impactor. It was a major boost to India's space capabilities, showcasing the country's ability to research and develop its own technology for lunar exploration. The mission successfully inserted into lunar orbit and contributed significantly to the understanding of the Moon's surface.
